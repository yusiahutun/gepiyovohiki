Tob Cohen: Widow Sarah Wairimu wants late tycoon's body handed to her

Publish date: 2024-02-09

- Tob Cohen's family indicated they would bury their kin according to Jewish culture

- Widow Wairimu wrote to Chirom Mortuary not to released the body to anyone else

- Wairimu who is chief suspect, maintained she was still legally married to him

- She differed with Cohen's sister who then stormed out of mortuary in rage

The family of late Dutch tycoon Tob Cohen may be headed for another court battle after his widow Sarah Wairimu demanded the body be handed to her once a postmortem is done.

Through her lawyer Philip Murgor, the widow asked Chiromo Mortuary management not to release Cohen's remains until murder case to identify his killers is heard and determined.

“The notion that the sister is entitled to the body is alien in the Kenyan Law…Sarah is innocent under the Kenyan Constitution and nothing has happened so far to take away her right to her husband’s body,” said Murgor.

Wairimu who is the chief suspect in the murder probe, noted she was still legally married to Cohen thus should be allowed to give him a befitting send off.

Cohen's sister Gabrielle initially indicated the family was considering burying their deceased kin in line with Jewish culture as soon as the autopsy results were out.

The two women bitterly clashed at the facility where postmortem examination to establish nature of the Dutch tycoon's death was being conducted.

A bitter Gabrielle then stormed out in rage following the disagreement.

The Dutch national went missing between July 19 and July 20 after filing a divorce case.
