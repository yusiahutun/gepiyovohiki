Renee Zellweger’s biography

The actress was born on April 25, 1969, in Katy, Texas. She is the daughter of Emil Erich Zellweger and Kjellfrid Irene. Her father was a mechanical and electrical engineer in the oil refining business, while her mother was a nurse and a midwife.

Renee attended her high school education at Katy High School in Texas. She was an active debater, cheerleader, and gymnast while at the school. She also played soccer and powder football.

In 1986 she wrote an academic paper, The Karankawas and Their Roots, which won third place in the first-ever Houston Post High School Natural Science Essay Contest. She attended the University of Texas in Austin, from where she graduated with a Bachelor of Arts Degree in English.
