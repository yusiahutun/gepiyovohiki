TUKO.co.ke lists milk companies in Kenya.

1. Brookside Dairy Ltd

According to its website, Brookside has been in operation for over 25 years.

The company buys raw milk from over 200,000 farmers and processes it. Besides Brookside, its other brands include Ilara, Tuzo, Molo Milk, and Delamere.

The Kenyatta family owns 50% of the company, while Danone controls a 40% stake. The rest, 10%, is owned by The Abraaj Group.

2. New Kenya Co-operative Creameries Ltd (New KCC)

The New KCC was founded in 1925, with Hugh Cholmondeley (Lord Delamere) serving as its first chairman.

It was registered in 1932 under the Co-operative Societies Ordinance.

In 2005, it was registered as a state corporation and renamed New Kenya Co-operative Creameries Ltd.

The company produces the KCC Mala, Gold Crown Premium Milk, KCC Fresh Milk, KCC UHT long life milk, and Safariland powder milk.

3. Bahati Dairies

Bahati Dairies was established in 2012 in Nakuru as a family business.

It manufactures the Bahati milk products that include yoghurts, maziwa lala and mozzarella cheese.

4. Milele Fresh

The dairy processing company is based in Kilimani, Nairobi with regional offices in Limuru and Yatta.

It manufactures the Milele Fresh milk. The company also produces yoghurt, butter, and cheese.

Milele Fresh is a sister company to Synergy Solutions Kenya Limited (Synergy LTD). Its chief executive officer is Dickson Muli.

5. Daima Africa

Daima is owned by Sameer Agriculture, its parent company.

According to its website, it boasts 50 million consumers, over 30 products, and employs over 500 people.

Under the Daima brand, it manufactures fresh milk, yoghurt, juice drink, chocolates, and smoothies.

6. Eldoville Dairies

The company was launched in 1985 by Lucy Karuga as a family business.

It sells milk products under the Eldoville brand.

In 2014, private Equity firm, Pearl Capital Partners, bought a minority stake at KSh 200 million.

7. Meru Dairy Co-operative Union Ltd

Meru Dairy Co-operative Union Ltd was established in 1985 to help Mt Kenya farmers market their produce.

It was formed by three co-operative societies namely Katheri, Naari, and Buuri.

It merged with the Coffee Union in 1972 to form the giant Meru Farmers Co-operative Union.

Its factory can process 500,000 liters of milk daily. The company processes Mt Kenya fresh milk, Mt Kenya yoghurt, and Mt Kenya ghee.

8. Kinangop Dairy Ltd

Kinangop Dairy Ltd formerly Mkulima Creameries Ltd was established in 1999.

It processes packages and distributes dairy products using milk produced from Kinangop catchment areas in Nyandarua county.

It produces the Kinangop milk brands.

9. Githunguri Dairy Farmers Cooperative Society Ltd

Data on its website indicate that the Githunguri Dairy Farmers Cooperative Society Ltd was formed in 1961 by 31 dairy farmers.
