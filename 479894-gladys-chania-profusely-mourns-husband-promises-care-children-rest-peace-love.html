Gladys Chania Profusely Mourns Husband, Promises to Take Care of Children: &quot;Rest in Peace My Love&quot;

Publish date: 2024-09-20
  • Kiambu politician Gladys Chania left many moved after mourning her husband in an emotional tribute during his burial
  • Chania detailed how she and her husband had been married for 24 years and brought forth three children into the world
  • Chania, who is the prime suspect in her husband's murder, promised to fulfil his wishes and look after their children

Kiambu politician Gladys Chania has emotionally mourned her husband, George Mwangi, in a heartwarming tribute during his burial at their palatial Mang'u home.

Chania mourned her husband of 24 years and whose murder she is the prime suspect during a burial ceremony held today, Thursday, October 27.

Gladys Chania's moving tribute

In the tribute read at the funeral by her younger sister Judy Wanjiku, Chania started by thanking her husband for his love and concern for their family for the 24 years that they were together.

"My husband, it has been 24 years since the day we settled together on Sunday, April 25, 1998. We have grown from two to five. I thank God for how your care and concern surrounded our family day in and day out," the tribute read.

Wanjiku, who was struggling to hold her tears, went on to read Chania's tribute in which she acknowledged that despite them having challenges in their family, they had love for one another.

Family members had to join in to comfort her as she continued reading.

"For now that you have left us untimely, we shall try to fit in your shoes, but it is already difficult to fill them. Brian wonders why you will not see him go to university. I promise to uphold all your wishes and see the success of your children. Rest in peace my love, the father of our children," Chania's sister read as her emotions ran high.

Gladys Chania released on bail

This came a few days after Gladys Chania and his co-accused were released after paying KSh 1 million bail with the condition of reporting to the investigating officer every morning.

The duo appeared at the Kiambu Law Court before Senior Resident Magistrate Wilson Rading at the Kiambu on Monday, October 24.

Officers from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations arrested the ex-Kiambu woman representative aspirant over the suspected involvement in killing her husband at their Mang'u home.
