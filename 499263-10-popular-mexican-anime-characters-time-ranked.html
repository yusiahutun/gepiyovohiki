10 popular Mexican anime characters of all time, ranked

Publish date: 2024-08-22

Anime is usually considered Japanese, which is understandable given that the art originated there. However, there is a lot of anime outside of Japan, including in countries such as Mexico. Anime in Mexico has been around for several decades, dating back to the early 1960s. However, it wasn’t until the 1980s and 90s that art became popular. Since then, several animations have been produced, becoming a hit in Mexico and internationally. But who are the most popular Mexican anime characters of all time?

Anime is a global phenomenon. Besides anime shows and films based in Mexico, several anime from around the world have also featured Mexican characters. As a result, there are several fan-favourite Mexican characters.

Top 10 Mexican anime characters

You can find many anime characters in animated films and TV shows. According to Fiction Horizon, here is a look at the most popular characters who have made headlines in the community.

1. Sado Yasutora

Sado Yasutora, also known as Chad, is one of the top Mexican anime characters. He appears in the Japanese anime show, Bleach. In the series, he is one of the best friends of Ichigo Kurosaki, the show’s main protagonist.

At the show's beginning, he is depicted as human but having incredible strength and endurance. Then, however, he develops supernatural abilities, becoming a powerful soul reaper. Later in the series, he becomes a professional boxer.

In the Bleach anime show, Sado is portrayed as being of Mexican and Japanese heritage. He has a dark skin tone, which he is proud of (as well as his Mexican heritage) due to how his grandfather raised him.

2. Leo de la Iglesia

Leo de la Iglesia is another popular Mexican anime character who appears on Yuri!!! On Ice, a Japanese sports anime show. He is a professional figure skater and choreographer from America. He is highly talented and won a gold medal in the Skate America competition.

He is also popular and well-liked with several fans in the skating community and on social media. On Yuri!!! On Ice, Leo is portrayed as being of Mexican-American descent. He has light brown skin with brown hair and eyes.

The series doesn't make a huge deal about his heritage, instead portraying it as simply part of who he is. However, he is a significant character, given that he is only one of the few American anime characters of Latino descent.

3. Nadia

Nadia is one of the most popular female anime characters of Mexican heritage. She is the main protagonist of the anime show El Cazador de la Bruja (Spanish for The Hunter of the Witch). The show is produced in Japan but set in Texas, with several scenes in Mexico.

Nadia (whose Spanish name means “nobody”) is a witch bounty hunter. However, she works as a bodyguard for Ellis, a budding witch, on a journey to discover her heritage. Nadie is hinted at as the sole survivor of an attack in her hometown in Mexico and is often depicted as having a quick temper. However, she is also fond and protective of Ellis.

4. Ricardo Martinez

Ricardo Martinez is a professional boxer of Mexican origin, and one of the main characters in the Japanese anime show Hajime No Ippo. He is portrayed as a powerful, talented boxer who is the undefeated WBA champion. Throughout the series, he faces off against several other fan-favourite characters, defeating them.

Ricardo is revealed to have grown up in Mexico, in the mountain regions of the Latin country, where his parents were mountain workers. He became interested in boxing as a child and fell in love with the sport, thus deciding to become a professional boxer. He is portrayed as popular in Mexico, even more than politicians.

5. Lola Gonzalez

Lola Gonzalez is another top female anime character of Mexican origin. She appears in the Japanese anime series School Rumble. She is introduced into the series as a transfer student from Mexico and quickly earns a reputation for being a tough and skilled wrestler.

6. Pepe Iglesias

Pepe Iglesia is another popular anime character of Mexican origin. He is a street fighter and professional boxer who appears in the anime show Megalo Box. He is shown to be a formidable and skilled fighter, making him an intimidating opponent in the ring.

Pepe's Mexican ethnicity is a huge part of his character. In the series, he only speaks Spanish and even uses the language to taunt his opponents. He also often wears Mexican sugar cubes as part of his persona.

7. Princess Maya

Princess Maya is an anime character appearing on Maya and The Three, one of the few Mexican anime shows on Netflix. The character, voiced by Zoe Saldana, is the main protagonist and plays the titular role in the series.

In the series, Maya is a Mesoamerica warrior princess forced by gods to embark on a journey to right the wrongs of her family. Three other warriors assist her in her quest to save her family and humanity.

Maya's heritage is based on the ancient Mexican societies – the Aztecs, Maya, and Inca. While the series does not refer to these cultures outrightly, its artwork and ideologies are heavily influenced by them.

8. Leonardo “Leo” San Juan

Leonardo San Juan, also known as Leo, is a character in La Leyenda (Spanish for The Legend), another Mexican anime on Netflix. He is introduced into the series as a meek and timid boy but later grows into a courageous hero who goes out of his way to help others.

9. Izel

Izel is one of the most popular male anime characters of Mexican origin. He appears in the Mexican-American anime show Onyx Equinox.

The series depicts him as a young, reluctant hero who saves humans by closing the underworld's gates. Izel’s ethnicity is Mexican. He was born in Tenochtitlan in the Aztec Empire (today’s Mexico city).

10. Peyote Diaz

Peyote Diaz, also known as Ramon, is a Mexican anime character from the Japanese show Shaman King. He is a shaman with powerful plant-based magical abilities. He is also able to see spirits and even has several guardian ghosts.

Peyote is of Mexican origin, having been born in Mexico. He has deep ties to the Mexican culture, with his mother being an Aztec priestess. His character is also often depicted wearing a traditional Mexican cloak.

What anime is popular in Mexico?

Anime is popular in Mexico due to several reasons. First, there is less censorship of anime films and shows in the country. Secondly, anime storylines share values similar to those of Mexican cultures, such as friendship, family, loyalty, bravery, and resilience.

There are several popular anime in Latin America, with Dragon Ball having the most fans. Other popular anime in Mexico include Sailor Moon, Digimon, One Piece, and Power Rangers.

What anime character is Afro-Latino?

Michiko Malandro is one of the most popular Afro-Latino anime characters. She is the main protagonist and the titular character of the Japanese anime Michiko & Hatchin. She is depicted as being Afro-Diamandran, with Diamandra being a Latin-American country, most likely Brazil.

The above are some of the most popular Mexican anime characters. They include characters from Mexican-based anime shows and Mexican characters in Japanese and other anime. And despite not being Japanese like most anime characters are, they have managed to capture the hearts of anime fans worldwide.
