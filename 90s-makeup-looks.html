The Best 90s Makeup Looks to Recreate

Publish date: 2024-04-26
1

Bitten-Red Lips

Steve Eichner//Getty Images

Just like cherry-red lips are indicative to the '80s, a bitten red lip traces back to the '90s. From matte to glossy, this deep hue was worn by the likes of Gwyneth Paltrow, Gwen Stefani, and Reese Witherspoon. In true '90s girl fashion, keep your base bright with a wash of pink blush.

Spider Lashes

Mick Hutson//Getty Images

Spider lashes may be a controversial trend. But the ultra-long lash look was beloved by '90s girls' who lived for heavy mascara. Luckily, they can easily be created sans-mascara. Try a set faux lashes that are clumped together, or add individual lashes in clusters to replicate that spider effect.

Pastel Lids

Ron Galella, Ltd.//Getty Images

As a change from neutral hues, celebs wore pastels all over the lid—from the lash line to just below the brow. When it comes to a pastels like lilac, just a swipe of mascara is needed to complete the eye, without overpowering the light, airy hue.

All-Around Tightline Eyeliner

Robin Platzer/Twin Images//Getty Images

From brows to eyeliner, the '90s era is all about thin lines—especially slim tightlined liner. Copy the look by rimming your upper and bottom lash lines with a kohl eyeliner (try a jet-black hue like Gwyneth) and set the liner with black eyeshadow to avoid raccoon eyes. This trend looks best when paired with neutral lips and blush.

Graphic Liner

Claudio Lavenia//Getty Images

Floating liner popped up on the Fall/Winter 2020 runways of designers like Prada and Valentino. But graphic liner was actually a major trend in the '90s. Take a cue from Bella Hadid and draw a straight line below your brow to create eye-catching negative space. You can also road-test the trend by tracing the outline of a cat-eye without filling it in.

Overlined Lips

Tim Roney//Getty Images

By now, we are all well aware that placing lip liner just outside our natural lip line creates the illusion of fuller lips. But celebs like Victoria Beckham pioneered the trick back in the 1990s. Start by overlining your lips with a dark nude or brown pencil. But instead of adding a matching lipstick on top, layering on a nude gloss for the ultimate 90s pout.

Blue Eyeshadow

Steve Granitz//Getty Images

It's hard to ignore blue eyes as a major trend of the 90s. Follow J. Lo's lead and use a darker blue on the outer corner and work towards a lighter hue in the inner corner and even on the lower lash line.

Glitter Shadow

Ron Galella, Ltd.//Getty Images

Glitter was an integral part of the 90s makeup – whether it be dusted on the décolleté, tapped onto the cheekbones, or, as J. Lo artfully shows us, worked into eyeshadow. And if the 1990s taught us anything, it's that sparkle goes with sparkle (so pack on the glitter makeup).
