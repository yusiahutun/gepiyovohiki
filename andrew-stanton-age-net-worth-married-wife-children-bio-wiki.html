Who is Andrew Christopher Stanton Jr.? Bio & Wiki

Andrew Stanton was born as Andrew Christopher Stanton Jr. on 3rd December 1965, in Rockport, Massachusetts, United States. Moreover, he holds an American by his nationality and belongs to Caucasian white ethnicity.

Stanton is a graduate of California Institute of the Arts (CalArts). From a very young age, Stanton had a keen interest in the field of entertainment. He also participated in many school programs and wrote many stories plays while at college. Later, he decided to pursue his career as a director and writer in the field of acting.

Inside of Andrew Stanton’s Career

Andrew Stanton started his career from 1987 short movie, Somewhere in the Arctic where he directed and voiced the role of Bahr. Later, he directed a Pixar animated movie, Light & Heavy in 1981. He got his big break when he wrote Toy Story in 1995. Moreover, he also directed and wrote an animated adventure comedy movie, A Bug’s Life in 1998. His other movie credits are Finding Nemo, Cars, and many others.

Stanton wrote an American animated television series, Mighty Mouse: The New Adventures for 13 episodes in 1987. He then produced an animated series, 2 Stupid Dogs in 1994. Furthermore, he directed the American animated drama series, The Lion King’s Timon & Pumbaa for an episode of Good Mousekeeping. His other television works are Better Call Saul and Stranger Things.

Stanton is currently portraying Hopper replacing Kevin Spacey in the 3rd movie, It’s Tough to Be a Bug since 1998. He also plays multiple roles in the play of Finding Nemo Submarine Voyage since 2007. He is working as a producer for two upcoming movies including Purl and Toy Story 4 in 2019.
