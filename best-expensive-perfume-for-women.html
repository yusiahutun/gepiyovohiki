What makes perfume so expensive?

The difference between a $20 perfume and a $200 perfume comes down to the design and raw materials used to craft the scent. A bottle that's flecked with gold or crystals and hand-crafted by an artisan glassmaker will, obviously, cost more than one far simpler. Many of the expensive fragrances on this last also utilize large amounts of expensive notes like rose, jasmine, and oud.

How-to shop for an expensive fragrance

Hearst
  • Look at the labels: While not all designer or fine fragrances will cost you a pretty penny, starting with a designer or heritage brand will more times than not mean a higher price tag. From there, think about the notes or scent families you love best.
  • Go custom: Take signature scent to a whole new level by going custom. Making a custom scent with a perfumer isn't going to come cheap, but you will end up with a creation that's unique to you that you can cherish and wear for years to come.
  • Think about the retailer: If you're at a high-end department store, chances are you're going to be around more expensive fragrance options. Experts there can help you test the waters to find the luxe perfume that's perfect for you.
  • Research perfumers: Creating a perfume is an art, a skill, and a science. Do a little research on some of the most sought-after perfumers and try out a few creations of theirs.
