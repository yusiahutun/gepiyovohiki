Cars line Carlee Russell's home on her birthday weekend as police continue to dig into her abduction

Publish date: 2024-01-29

Cars were seen lining up outside Carlee Russell's Alabama home on Sunday, just a day after her 26th birthday.

There was no official word on why all the cars were at the home of the woman who has become a central player in a missing person case that has captivated the nation. 

A dozen cars lined the driveway and around the home, and drew the attention of police in Hoover, Alabama, who drove past the home as people entered and left.

Police said Russell delayed additional requests to interview her again about her supposed July 13 disappearance. Russell vanished after calling 911 to report a toddler wandering along an Alabama highway. 

That sparked a national hunt for her that ended 49 hours later when she returned to her family's home. 

Authorities say they have not found any evidence to corroborate the woman's claims and are now investigating her story.

Friends and family gathered at Russell's Alabama home on Sunday, just one day after her 26th birthday

Police in Hoover, Alabama, are investigating claims that Russell was kidnapped on July 13, and have said they have not found evidence to back those claims

Russell has claimed she left work around 8.20pm on July 13 before stopping to pick up food for her and her mom. 

She then travelled toward Hoover and called 911 around 9.30pm to report the missing child wandering on the side of the Alabama interstate.

After calling 911, Russell reportedly called her sister-in-law to tell her she was going to check up on the child. The family member lost contact with her at about 9:36 pm but the line remained open. 

Responding officers located Russell's abandoned car along with her cell phone, her purse, wig and apple watch in the nearby area, but no sign of her or a child.  

Hoover Police later said they had not received any other calls of a missing child. 

A witness reported possibly seeing a gray vehicle and a man standing outside of Carlee's vehicle, but police said they have no additional information. 

After reappearing at her parents' house two days later, Carlee told detectives she was abducted by a man with orange hair who held her captive for two days.

She was then able to escape, Russell claims. 

The nursing student is now said to be cooperating with authorities, but police said they have found no proof there was a toddler on the side of the road, nor of the abduction tale she spun them.

That has led some to accuse her of faking the entire ordeal.

Authorities said on Sunday that the more than $63,000 raised during the two-day search for Russell will not be returned. 

Crime Stoppers initially said the money would be returned to donors when Russell showed up at her parent's home on July 15.

Now the organization has backtracked on this promise because the investigation is 'still ongoing'.

Crime Stoppers announced on Sunday they will not be returning  the more than $63,000 raised during the two-day search for Russell

'This investigation is still ongoing, and accordingly, there is no basis to refund any contributions at this time,' the organization wrote.

'Furthermore, the Hoover Police Department has not requested for any donor contributions to be released or refunded.'

The announcement served as an abrupt about-face from a statement delivered by  the agency's executive director Bob Copus just a few hours earlier, in which he said that an unspecified amount of donations had already been returned amid lingering doubts over the case's legitimacy.

Other payments, he said, were in the process of being refunded - if that was what the individual donor requested. 

As of Sunday, it was not it is not clear how much of the money was actually returned.

DailyMail.com has reached out to the Hoover Police Department for comment.

Russell's boyfriend Thomar Lattrel Simmons - who initially took to Facebook to ask for help searching for the missing woman - appears to have distanced himself from his beloved.

After a Hoover Police Department press conference on July 19 - two days after the Crimestoppers announcements - Thomar scrubbed all mention of Carlee from his Instagram and Facebook accounts.

He has not only deleted his posts about her disappearance, but has also erased every photo of her and them together.

On Saturday, Simmons pleaded for people to 'stop bullying' the nursing student. 

'The only thing I can say is, I want everyone to stop bullying her. I know what it seems like what she did. Just stop bullying on social media,' boyfriend Thomar Lattrel Simmons told the New York Post. 

'Think about her mental health. She doesn't deserve that. She doesn't. Nobody deserves to be cyberbullied' he added.
