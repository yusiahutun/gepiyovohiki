Disney Mouseketeer Annette Funicello rushed to hospital after flames engulf her LA home

Publish date: 2024-05-21

By David Gardner for MailOnline
Updated: 21:21 EDT, 10 March 2011

View
comments


Annette Funicello was one of the stars of The Mickey Mouse Club before forging an acting and singing career

Former Disney Mouseketeer Annette Funicello was rushed to hospital earlier today after a fire broke out at her California home.

The wheelchair-bound actress has been in declining health since being diagnosed more than 20 years ago with multiple sclerosis.

She was being treated for smoke inhalation following the 7.21 am blaze at her house in Encino.

The 68-year-old is best known for her years on ‘The Mickey Mouse Club’ and later as Frankie Avalon’s bikini-clad co-star in a spate of beach movies in the early 1960s.

Her husband and nurse were also taken to hospital. Nobody was seriously injured, according to witnesses.

It took nearly 30 minutes to control the flames in the 3,600-square foot home that was extensively damaged.

Among the personal possessions damaged by smoke were the star’s collection of movie posters.

Devastation: The fire ripped through her Los Angeles home destroying some of her movie posters

Firefighters were called to the home this morning. Funicello, who is in a wheelchair, was taken to hospital suffering smoke inhalation

Fire chiefs have not yet revealed the cause of the fire.

Funicello was discovered by Walt Disney when she performed in Swan Lake at a recital near the company’s movie studios in Burbank, California, when she was twelve.

She was cast as one of the original Mouseketeers and proved the most popular, receiving about 6,000 fan letters a month. Britney Spears and Christina Aguilera would later owe their big breaks to being picked as 'Mickey Mouse Club' cast members.

Annette Funicello has been in a wheelchair for nearly 20 years after suffering from multiple sclerosis. She starred in the iconic movie Beach Party

After making several pop hits, Funicello became a teen idol in iconic beach movies like ‘Beach Party’, ‘Muscle Beach Party’ and Beach Blanket Bingo.’

After battling multiple sclerosis in private for several years, she went public with the fact that she had the disease in 1992. The following year, she opened the Annette Funicello Fund for Neurological Disorders at the California Community Foundation.
