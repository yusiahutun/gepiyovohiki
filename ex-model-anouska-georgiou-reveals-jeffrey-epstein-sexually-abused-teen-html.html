Ex-model Anouska De Georgiou reveals how Jeffrey Epstein sexually abused her as a teen

Publish date: 2024-05-18

British actress and former model Anouska De Georgiou has described how she was manipulated and sexually abused by pedophile Jeffrey Epstein.

De Georgiou, who grew in an affluent family and went to school with Duchess of Cambridge Kate Middleton at Marlborough College, said she met Epstein in the Nineties through well-connected friends when he was in London . 

Epstein soon began to fly her to his properties across the globe, including New York, Paris and his Caribbean home, dubbed 'Orgy Island' by locals.   

In an interview with NBC on Dateline Friday, De Georgiou said Epstein's grooming was subtle, but eventually all consuming. 

De Georgiou (pictured) revealed how Epstein manipulated and sexually abused her as a teenager

She said: 'By the time I was being raped, it was too late.'

'And in every location there was this microcosm of acceptance, of yes people, who acted like this was normal.' 

She told NBC that Epstein abused her for several years and his enablers often turned a blind eye.  

'If you're a young person walking into a mansion or someone's island and all the people who are present are acting as though this is OK and you're the only one who thinks it's weird, it's hard to say something.'

De Georgiou as a teenager (pictured) met Epstein through friends while he was visiting London

Jeffrey Epstein (pictured) was arrested in July 2019 for sex trafficking with minors and died by suicide inside his holding cell in August

De Georgiou recalled Epstein having visceral reactions at the mere sight of her while he sexually abused her as a teenager. 

 'When Jeffrey would see me he would physically shake because he wanted to get at me and that was very unnerving,' she said. 

But the billionaires grooming tactics didn't happen all at once. 

'It wasn't zero to 100 in one day. It was introduced piecemeal, along with constant emotional, financial reinforcement that this was the path, the only path.'

She describes Epstein as being able to target her insecurities and vulnerabilities. 

'For me, I was involved in that kind of lifestyle growing up,' De Georgiou said, 'and so I thought I could cope. And I thought I could handle it.'   

De Georgiou recalls seeing other young girls lured into Epstein's abuse, in particular  seeing another young blonde while at his private Caribbean island.

She told NBC that the girl looked a few years younger than herself. 

De Georgiou (pictured): 'I was a victim, and it has taken me many, many years to stand here and say, yes, it was me'

De Georgiou (pictured) now works at The Kintsugi Foundation, a group home for women suffering from trauma or battling substance addiction

'I wanted to say something.'

'I was in the ocean and she was on a paddleboard or something...and I wanted to say, "You have to leave." But I didn't.'

De Georgiou now works at The Kintsugi Foundation, a group home for women suffering from trauma or battling substance addiction. 

'My healing came through that, that I could walk alongside these women and girls and be a witness to their suffering.'

Last month De Georgiou publicly identified herself as one of Epstein's victims during a federal court hearing in New York. 

She said: 'I was a victim, and it has taken me many, many years to stand here and say, yes, it was me.'

In August, De Georgiou and fifteen of Epstein's victims urged a federal judge to investigate the pedophile's alleged co-conspirators like Ghislaine Maxwell (pictured)

'I was a victim, but I will not remain a victim and be silent for one more day.'

Fifteen of Epstein's victims, including De Georgiou, were urging District Judge Richard Berman to investigate Epstein's alleged co-conspirators, including Ghislaine Maxwell.

Her statements came two weeks after Epstein was found dead inside his holding cell at the Metropolitan Correctional Center in New York on August 10. 

He reportedly hanged himself in an apparent suicide.

Epstein was previously arrested for sex trafficking with minors in July and was awaiting trial. 

'Although I think it’s tragic when anybody dies before their time, I’m extremely relieved that Jeffrey Epstein will not be in a position to hurt anymore children or anymore women.' 

Epstein accuser Virginia Roberts Giuffre (pictured) is one of the victims sharing her story on NBC's Dateline special 

'And I’m glad to be part of a group of women who are now bonded forever in the trauma that we endured at the hands of this man.' 

Epstein accuser Virginia Roberts Giuffre was also at the court house with De Georgiou. 

In the upcoming special, she goes into detail about her allegation that Ghislaine Maxwell orchestrated sex between her and Prince Andrew when she was 17.  

Prince Andrew has repeatedly and ardently denied the claims. 

Maxwell has also denied any involvement in coordinating Epstein's activities. 

A group of Epstein's accusers (pictured) appeared on NBC's Dateline to detail their stories

The Dateline special also includes Jennifer Araoz, Chante Davies and Rachel Benavidez.

De Georgiou said: 'No one else understands like somebody who’s experienced it.'

'Jeffrey thought that we were disposable and he threw us all away,' De Georgiou said, And look who’s standing.'
