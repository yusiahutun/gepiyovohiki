Gaming vlogger has Twitch account suspended due to 'accidental' nip slip

Publish date: 2024-07-29

A cotrovertial online gamer has been forced into the spotlight after a possible nudity violation on her live stream channel, reports news.com.au

Colombian eSports gamer star Natalia “Alinity” Mogollon has been in trouble before as she’s feuded with bloggers and faced accusations of animal cruelty.

Natalia, who goes by the name “Alinity Divine” lives in Canada and is in hot water having possibly staged a “nip slip” moment on her channel.

The gamer, who uses Twitch to livestream herself playing video games, tries to push a pillow up her shirt during a session of “Just Dance”.

Unfortunately, the moment caused her left nipple to become exposed – violating Twitch’s nudity policy.

The popularity of online gaming has grown since the world went into lockdown and after two months away from eSports, Alinity claims it is this which made her return to the platform.

“I just wanted a fun night of dancing and memes.

“Not to give more fire on the flame or however the f*** you say that, but I got one more thing to say.

“You guys knew I was Latina (yet) you expected me to have some nice pink nips – what the f***?”

She continued on her tirade: “If I had done it on purpose I would have made them look so much better.

“That lightning and angle didn’t do them any justice… no way that was on purpose.

“Bras f***ing suck, sl*ts rule, I love my body too much to let people’s opinions affect how I feel about it.”

Alinity, 32, was sanctioned for 24 hours by the Amazon-owned platform, but some users felt this wan’t enough.

Her wardrobe difficulty isn’t the first controversy the gamer has faced.

Alinity has a long-standing feud with PewDiePie – a blogging celebrity who once held the title of the most subscribed to channel on YouTube.

Swedish PewDiePie, real name Felix Arvid Ulf Kjellberg, has over 104 million subscribers and is estimated to be worth over £23m.

The feud involved Felix calling the 32-year-old a “Twitch th*t” on his livestream.

In response she asked her followers if she should “copy strike” the star.

This is slang for “copyright strike” and involves YouTube channels losing their monetisation due to infringements.

Alinity claims that Felix’s comments degrade women and are evidence of the “rampant sexism in online communities.”

Alinity has also faced the wrath of PETA and other animal rights groups for the treatment of her three cats, Maya, Milo And Nova.

In July last year, one of her cats interrupted her livestream and she aggressively threw the cat over her shoulder without looking so that she could resume the game.

After the incident, she said: “I didn’t think you guys would notice it, honestly.

“I thought I was fast enough that nobody would notice it – the cat flying around the screen – but I guess everybody did.”

In another incident Alinity takes a mouthful of vodka and allows a different cat to lick the substance off her mouth.

Alinity said in the video: “Vodka kiss, see, it is vodka.”

PETA accused her of “using the platform for animal abuse” and called for her removal.

She was investigated by the Saskatoon Society for the Prevention of Cruelty but they found her actions were not malicious.

Alinity wrote to her 316k Twitter followers: “I don’t know what the future has in store for me, but I welcome it with open arms: the success and failures, the love and hate, the beautiful and ugly.

“I will do my best to learn from my mistakes and tackle whatever comes next. I am not done yet.”

Alinity currently has around 1.08m followers on Twitch and over 63m views.
