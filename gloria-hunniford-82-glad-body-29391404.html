Gloria Hunniford, 82, 'glad her body still works' after having same job since 1969

Publish date: 2024-06-08

Loose Women star Gloria Hunniford has praised her body for pushing her through more than 50 years of work.

The TV legend has been in the industry since 1969, starting out in shows like Blankety Blank, now hosted by Bradley Walsh, and Call My Bluff.

Speaking with Lorraine Kelly about her latest body positive campaign, the 82-year-old admitted she was simply happy her "body still functions" and tries not to judge herself too much.

Gloria went on to reveal that she's struggled with her body weight in the past, explaining that she dropped a lot of weight after giving birth.

"When I was young, my standard weight was 7.2 stone and I was 6.10 stone after I gave birth to Karen my first baby," she told viewers. "Body issues then wasn't really any issue."

She added: "I'm just glad that my body is still functioning! I know there's some people that get out of the bath and look in the mirror and say, 'Oh I've got to get that, that and that done,' but I don't do that anymore,

"I'm all about having the energy in my body to do things I want to do ... like working, I'm still doing the job I started in 1969!"

Gloria is working on the ‘Celebrating Every Body’ campaign with Loose Women co-stars Carol McGiffin, Dame Kelly Holmes, Judi Love, Katie Piper OBE and Sophie Morgan.

Her work on the campaign comes after she starred on ITV's bizarre singing competition, The Masked Singer, where she appeared as character Snow Leopard.

Speaking about the show, Gloria told Lorraine: "Viewers don't understand how much secrecy surrounds the show,"

"I had to wear a black hoodie which read, 'Don't speak to me,' and a balaclava," she explained. "It was hard for me as an Irish woman because I love to talk!"
