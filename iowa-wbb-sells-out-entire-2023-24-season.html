Hawkeyes sell out entire 2023-24 season at Carver-Hawkeye Arena

Publish date: 2024-05-25

Season tickets for the Iowa women’s basketball team are sold out, the athletics department announced Monday. It is the first time in school history the program has sold out Carver-Hawkeye Arena. 

For context, Iowa has sold out three regular season games in program history with the last coming against Indiana in the 2022-23 finale.

The Hawkeyes will open the season hosting “Crossover at Kinnick” against DePaul in a scrimmage inside Kinnick Stadium on October 15 at 3 p.m.

Tickets for “Crossover at Kinnick” will go on sale to women’s basketball season ticket holders at 9 a.m. Tuesday to I-Club contributors and football season ticket holders at 9 a.m. on Wednesday and to the general public at 9 a.m. on Thursday.  

There is a $20 “Rain or Shine” ticket for reserved seat tickets at Kinnick with an option for Carver-Hawkeye Arena if weather forces the game indoors. There is also a $10 adult/$5 student and youth reserved ticket for Kinnick Stadium only.

The Hawkeyes won a school-record 31 games, finished as NCAA national runners-up and were second in the country in attendance. Senior guard Caitlin Clark, who was the consensus National Player of the Year and Collegiate Woman of the Year, averaged 27.8 points, 8.6 assists and 7.1 rebounds per game. Seniors Gabbie Marshall and Kate Martin and sophomore Hannah Stuelke also return for Lisa Bluder’s 24th season as Iowa’s head coach.
