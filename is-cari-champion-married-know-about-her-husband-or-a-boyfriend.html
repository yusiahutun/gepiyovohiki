Quick Facts About Cari Champion

  • Born in June 1975 in Pasadena, California.
  • Attended the University of California, Los Angeles.
  • She chose her career in the field of journalism with goals of transforming negative stereotypes of African Americans.
  • Earned an internship at CNN.
  • Graduated from UCLA back in the year 1998 with an English degree.
  • Before joining WGCL-TV in West Palm Beach, Florida, she was a reporter at the Orange County News Channel.
  • While working as an anchor for WGCL-TV in Atlanta, Champion she was fired for speaking foul language over the air in 2007. However, she was rehired in January 2008.
  • She developed a great interest in tennis after covering players such as Venus and Serena Williams.
  • Also worked as an entertainment reporter for the Starz network and shows such as The Insider and Hollywood 441.
  •  Champion joined ESPN as the new host of ESPN's  First Take on 1 October 2012 and hired Heidi Watney and Jemele Hill for the job.
  • Was later promoted as an anchor SportsCenter in 2015
  • Is a great fan of the Los Angeles Lakers and the UCLA Bruins.
  • Has an estimated net worth of about $2 million with an annual salary of $1 million.
