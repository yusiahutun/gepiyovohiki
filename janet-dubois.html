JaNet DuBois

Publish date: 2024-03-16

Ja’Net DuBois was an American actress, singer-songwriter, and dancer who went by the stage name Jeannette Dubois. She was most recognized for her role as Willona Woods on the comedy Good Times, in which she played the neighborhood gossip queen and a friend of the Evans family from 1974 to 1979. She also co-wrote and sang the theme song “Movin’ on Up” for the television show The Jeffersons, which ran from 1975 until 1985. Let us learn more about her by reading this article.

When did Ja’Net Dubios begin her acting career?

Moving on to her profession, DuBois began her acting career in the theater in the early 1960s, starring alongside Sammy Davis Jr. and Louis Gossett Jr. in Broadway’s Golden Boy in 1964.
Her early television acting credits include the 1969 television film J.T. and the long-running television soap opera Love of Life, where she played Loretta Allen from 1970 to 1972, making her the first African–American woman to star in a daytime serial.
She made her cinematic debut in 1970, as Vera in Diary of a Mad Housewife.
She was the founder and director of the Ja’net DuBois Academy of Theater Arts and Sciences on Long Island, New York, in the 1980s.
Maya Angelou’s play And I Still Rise, which she co-starred in alongside Clifton Davis in 1992, was written and directed by her. She appeared in I’m Gonna Git You Sucka (1988) and Charlie’s Angels: Full Throttle (2003), as well as Moesha, The Steve Harvey Show, A Different World, and The Wayans Bros. on television.
Her portrayal in the Lifetime film Other Women’s Children earned her a CableACE award for Best Supporting Actress in 1995. In 1999 and 2001, she received Emmy Awards for her voice-over work on the animated series The PJs.
At the 2006 TV Land Awards, she and the cast of Good Times got The Impact Icon Award.
She was the Grand Marshal of the North Amityville Community Parade and Festival Day in Amityville, New York, in the year 2000.
