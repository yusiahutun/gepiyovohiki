Early Life of Julie Banderas

Julie Banderas was born on September 25, 1973, in Hartford, Connecticut to Fabiola R., a Colombian woman, and Howard D. Bidwell. She is American and belongs to the White ethnicity. Banderas gained her bachelor's degree from Emerson College.

Julie Banderas' Career

Julie Banderas started her TV career at WLVI-TV in Boston, Massachusetts. Later, she joined the Fox News Channel as a general assignment reporter. After the year of joining Fox, she gained an international media attention for her infamous on-air clash with Shirley Phelps-Roper who is a spokeswoman for the infamous Westboro Baptist Church.

In 2008, she was replaced by Laurie Dhue for her position in the channel. She took maternity leave in 2010 and made her come back at the end of the year.
