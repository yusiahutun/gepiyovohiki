Biography Timeline

Karine Jean-Pierre is a political campaign organizer, political analyst, and commentator who has worked with several Democratic politicians. She is the principal deputy press secretary for the White House under the Biden administration. Here is a timeline of her life and career:

  • 1977: Karine Jean-Pierre was born in Martinique, a French territory in the Caribbean Sea.
  • 1994-1998: Jean-Pierre attends the New York City Lab School for Collaborative Studies and graduates with honours.
  • 1998-2002: Jean-Pierre attends Columbia University, where she graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science.
  • 2002-2003: Jean-Pierre works as a legislative fellow for the New York City Council.
  • 2003-2004: Jean-Pierre is a regional political director for Howard Dean’s presidential campaign.
  • 2004-2005: Jean-Pierre is the deputy chief of staff for John Edwards’ vice-presidential campaign.
  • 2006-2007: Jean-Pierre is the campaign manager for Rep. Ted Strickland’s successful gubernatorial campaign in Ohio.
  • 2007-2011: Jean-Pierre works as the southeast regional political director for the Obama for America campaign and later as the national deputy battleground states director for Obama’s re-election campaign.
  • 2011-2014: Jean-Pierre served as the deputy press secretary for the White House Office of Legislative Affairs under the Obama administration.
  • 2014-2016: Jean-Pierre is the national spokesperson and director of communications for the left-leaning advocacy group MoveOn.org.
  • 2016-2021: Jean-Pierre is a political analyst and commentator for MSNBC and NBC News.
  • 2021-Present: Jean-Pierre is appointed the principal deputy press secretary for the White House under the Biden administration.

Throughout her career, Jean-Pierre has advocated for progressive policies, including healthcare reform, climate change action, and LGBTQ rights. She is also a lecturer at Columbia University’s School of International and Public Affairs.
