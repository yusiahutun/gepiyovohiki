Leslie's Career and Salary:

Leslie began her career in 2010 at Fox Business Network as a booker. In 2011, she joined Bloomberg TV as a reporter where she covered initial public offerings.

In 2015, Leslie became a deals reporter in New York Times where she covered shareholder activism, restructuring, mergers and acquisitions, private equity and initial public offerings.

Afterwards, she joined CNBC in the post of a reporter in 2017 where she covered reports on hedge funds, private equity, and asset management. Throughout her professional life, she had taken interviews with many investors like Kenneth Griffin, David Einhorn, Leon Cooperman, and Bill Ackman.

In an interview, Leslie shared her experiences about being a journalist. According to her,

“I get to be insatiably curious for a living. The basis for being a journalist is asking questions, getting answers and synthesizing those answers so that they’re easy to understand for a broader audience. One of the things that attracted me to business journalism is that there are some much complexity and nuance.”
