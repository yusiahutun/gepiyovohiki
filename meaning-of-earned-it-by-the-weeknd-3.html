Meaning of Earned It by The Weeknd

Publish date: 2024-05-31

The Weeknd's hit song "Earned It" is a mesmerizing blend of soulful vocals and sensual lyrics that delve into the themes of love, desire, and the complexities of relationships. The song, which gained popularity as the lead single from the 2015 film "Fifty Shades of Grey" soundtrack, captures the essence of the film's provocative storyline. In this article, we will analyze the standout lyrics from "Earned It" and explore the unexpected and imaginative themes that they help develop within the song.The opening verse sets the tone for the song, with The Weeknd singing, "You make it look like it's magic, 'cause I see nobody, nobody but you, you, you. I'm never confused." These lyrics introduce the overarching theme of infatuation and the inexplicable attraction that the narrator feels towards their love interest. It suggests that when love is genuine and all-encompassing, it begins to feel like pure magic. The notion of being "never confused" implies a deep connection and understanding between the two individuals. This theme of undeniable magnetism is a recurring element throughout the song.Within the pre-chorus, The Weeknd croons, "So I love when you call unexpected, 'cause I hate when the moment's expected." These lyrics speak to the thrill and excitement that comes from spontaneous gestures in a relationship. It highlights the importance of keeping the flame alive by constantly surprising and challenging one another. The theme of unexpectedness suggests that love is dynamic and ever-evolving; it should never be taken for granted. By emphasizing the power of unpredictability, the song explores the concept of romantic escapades and the desire to break free from societal norms.The chorus of "Earned It" holds some of the most memorable lyrics, including, "Girl, you're perfect, you're always worth it, and you deserve it, the way you work it." These lines celebrate the idea of self-worth and female empowerment. The narrator expresses admiration and adoration for their love interest, attributing qualities of perfection and deservingness. By recognizing and validating the efforts put into the relationship, the song underscores the importance of acknowledging and appreciating one's partner. These lyrics promote the idea that both individuals in a relationship deserve love and respect, reinforcing the theme of equality and empowerment.The second verse delves into the notion of honesty in a romantic relationship, with The Weeknd singing, "You know our love would be tragic, so you don't pay it, don't pay it no mind, mind, mind. We live with no lies." Here, the song recognizes the flaws and potential complications that can arise in a relationship. However, rather than dwelling on the negatives, the lyrics emphasize the need for acceptance and understanding. By choosing to live truthfully and authentically, the couple in the song demonstrates a commitment to transparency and emotional growth. This theme resonates with the idea that love requires effort and maturity to navigate successfully.The final verse presents an unexpected twist by alluding to the secretive nature of the relationship, singing, "On that lonely night, we said it wouldn't be love, but we felt the rush. It made us believe it was only us, convinced we were broken inside, yeah, inside, yeah." These lyrics introduce a theme of intimacy and secrecy, hinting at a forbidden love that transcends societal expectations. The protagonist acknowledges the allure of a relationship that is kept hidden from the world, where two individuals find solace in each other's presence. This theme explores the vulnerability and challenges that can arise when love defies conventions.In conclusion, The Weeknd's "Earned It" delves into the various dimensions of love, desire, and the complexities of relationships. The song's standout lyrics contribute to the development of unexpected and imaginative themes that revolve around infatuation, unpredictability, self-worth, honesty, and forbidden love. By exploring these themes, the song offers listeners a thought-provoking perspective on love and relationships that goes beyond traditional notions. "Earned It" serves as a reminder that love is a multifaceted journey, often requiring effort, understanding, and an appreciation for the unexpected.
