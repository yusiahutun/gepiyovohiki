Meaning of This What I Call Her by Lil Wayne

Publish date: 2024-06-04

In the rap game, Lil Wayne is renowned for his catchy hooks and clever wordplay. One particular song that showcases his lyrical prowess is "This What I Call Her." This track delves into the realm of relationships, highlighting the complexities and desires we often experience when connecting with someone. While the song's explicit language might deter some listeners, there are several standout lyrics that, when analyzed, reveal unexpected and imaginative themes.From the outset, Lil Wayne contemplates whether or not to call a girl he recently met. This dilemma presents the overarching theme of the song - the struggle to navigate the early stages of a romantic relationship. Should he be sweet and endearing, or should he adopt a more aggressive and direct approach?One standout lyric that develops this theme is when Lil Wayne mentions the girl's name, Chantel, and how she claims to have a man. This lyric introduces the theme of temptation and infidelity. It suggests that while Chantel may be committed to someone else, the allure and chemistry between her and Lil Wayne are undeniable. This theme highlights the inherent tension and moral ambiguity present in relationships.Furthermore, Lil Wayne compares Chantel to a variety of sweet and enticing treats, such as Butter Pecan and caramel. This metaphorical exploration develops the theme of indulgence and desire. Just like these delicious flavors, Lil Wayne finds himself irresistibly drawn to Chantel. The choice of food-related imagery also suggests that the connection between them is something he craves and finds satisfying.Another intriguing lyric is when Lil Wayne describes jellyfish and seashells while referring to moments shared with Chantel. This unexpected imagery conjures up the idea of a hidden underwater world full of mystery and enchantment. It symbolizes the allure of exploring the unknown and discovering new aspects of oneself through the exploration of another person. This theme speaks to the human desire for growth and discovery within relationships.In a similar vein, Lil Wayne mentions a "late night" phone call and the actions that follow. This lyric creates a sense of urgency and secrecy, building on the previously introduced theme of temptation and infidelity. It highlights the clandestine nature of their interaction and adds a layer of excitement and danger to their connection. This theme explores the transient and thrilling nature of certain relationships that exist outside the bounds of societal norms.Towards the end of the song, Lil Wayne boldly declares that he is "too much." This lyric introduces the theme of self-confidence and assertiveness. It implies that Lil Wayne's charisma and bravado are part of the appeal that draws Chantel towards him. This theme challenges conventional ideas of attraction and suggests that sometimes, being brash and unapologetic can engender desire and interest.Ultimately, "This What I Call Her" delves into the multifaceted nature of relationships. It explores themes of temptation, desire, indulgence, secrecy, growth, and self-confidence. By weaving these themes together, Lil Wayne offers an intricate and imaginative portrayal of the complexities we encounter when navigating the romantic landscape. While the explicit nature of the song may be off-putting to some, a closer examination reveals a rich tapestry of themes that captivate the listener's imagination.
