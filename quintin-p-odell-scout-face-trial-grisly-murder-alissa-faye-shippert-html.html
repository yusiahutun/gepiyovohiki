An Eagle Scout has been accused of hacking a young woman to death and disemboweling another.

Quintin P O'Dell, 22, will stand trial for murder and assault following the alleged incidents in Missouri. He denies both charges.

The body of Alissa Faye Shippert, who he worked with at a convenience store, was found in a conservation area where she had been fishing.

He is also charged with slashing open the stomach of a 21-year-old woman he had been drinking with on Christmas Day.

Violence: Quintin P. O'Dell said he was drinking with one of his victims when he became 'enraged' and cut her stomach open with a razor

The investigation into the hatchet attack began in spring after the body of 22-year-old Shippert, who was attacked while fishing in the Platte Falls Conservation Area, was discovered.

O’Dell was not charged with the crime until after he was questioned in the December razor attack.

The victim in that incident, a 21-year-old woman, awoke in her apartment the morning after Christmas with her belly slashed open.

Authorities said she was unconscious and on a ventilator for several days after the attack. 

First-degree murder: Alissa Faye Shippert, 22, was hacked to death while fishing in Missouri's Platte Falls Conservation Area

Court documents said she had spent Christmas night drinking with O’Dell, who had called her asking to 'hang out'.

She agreed and he arrived after 11 pm with a six-pack of beer and a bottle of tequila. 

WHAT IS AN EAGLE SCOUT?

Eagle Scout is the highest rank attainable in the Boy Scouting movement in the U.S.

The lifetime title was introduced in 1911, is held by more than two million young men and comes once the Court has earned at least 21 merit badges.

They must also demonstrate Scout Spirit through the Boy Scout Oath and Law, service, and leadership.

It includes an extensive service project that the Scout plans, organises, leads, and manages.

Eagle Scouts are presented with a medal and a badge that visibly recognises their accomplishments.

But he 'became enraged' after listening to her arguing on the phone with her recent boyfriend about the future of their relationship.

O’Dell, during interview, reportedly showed detectives how he cut open the victim using a razor he had seen earlier on her kitchen counter.

During the same interviews, according to the documents, O’Dell also disclosed details of the May 31 hatchet attack on Shippert.

He said he walked alongside the Platte River that day and found the hatchet on the bank. He came upon Shippert and spent two hours talking to her while she fished.

He told detectives the attack happened after he lost his footing in the river as he was trying to help her free her snagged line. He resurfaced downstream and walked back.

But he said Shippert thought he had drowned and began slapping him for frightening her. 

Crime scene: Police divers searched the river where the body of Alissa Faye Shippert, 22, was discovered. She had been attacked while fishing in the Platte Falls Conservation Area

He told deputies he grabbed her in a bear hug to control her but that she began slapping him again when he released her.

'O’Dell claims that both he and Shippert reached for his hatchet on his canteen belt,' the probable cause statement said.

'O’Dell pulled the hatchet away from Shippert and remembered striking her in the back of the head with the hatchet blade side while she was standing up.'

O’Dell said he then decided to end Shippert’s suffering and 'repeatedly struck her in the face with the hatchet until she was no longer crying or breathing.'

Afterwards, he said he decided to drown himself but lost his footing as he entered the river. As he fell, he said, he grabbed Shippert’s body, pulling it into the river with him. He then let go of her body and the hatchet.

O’Dell said he returned to his mother’s home, where he was living, by floating down the river, according to the document.

O'Dell is jailed in Platte County on a $750,000 cash-only bond. He is requesting a public defender.

Prosecutor Eric Zahnd said the death penalty would be considered in the Platte County Circuit Court case.
