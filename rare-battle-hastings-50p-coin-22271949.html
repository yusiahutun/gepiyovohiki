Rare Battle of Hastings 50p coin sells for 63k you could have one hiding away

Publish date: 2024-04-09

Are you a rare coin collector?

A Battle of Hastings 50p coin has just sold on eBay for a whopping £63,100.

After a price war among collectors, the piece of change was flogged for more than 126,000 times its face value.

The coin, which marks the Battle of Hastings fought in 1066, is one of 6,700,000 in circulation.

To mark 950 years since the famous battle, the 50p coins came into circulation back in 2016.

It signifies the historical battle between King Harold II of England and William the Conqueror.

The back of the coin features the drawing of King Harold with an arrow through his eye taken from the Bayeux Tapestry.

It was put online with a starting offer of £1,500 which swiftly rose to more than £60,000 over the seven days it was listed for.

And by the time the auction ended, it garnered 41 bids.

Despite the value of these coins, there are other rare ones out there to be found.
