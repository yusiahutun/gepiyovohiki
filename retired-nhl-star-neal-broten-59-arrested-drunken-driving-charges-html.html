Retired NHL star Neal Broten, 59, arrested on drunken driving charges

Publish date: 2024-06-21

The 59-year-old Broten was taken into custody on Sunday night. He was released from jail on Monday morning after being cited for his third drunken driving offense, as well as possessing open intoxicants in a motor vehicle and failure to stop at a stop sign

Neal Broten, a member of America's gold medal-winning 'Miracle on Ice' Olympic hockey team and a former NHL player, was arrested on suspicion of drunken driving near his western Wisconsin home.

The 59-year-old was taken into custody on Sunday night, according to The St. Paul Pioneer Press. He was released from jail on Monday morning after being cited for his third drunken driving offense, as well as possessing open intoxicants in a motor vehicle and failure to stop at a stop sign.

He was reportedly arrested for drunken driving in 1995 and 2003. 

Broten is expected back in the Pierce County Courthouse on July 8, according to Wisconsin Circuit Court Access.

The Pioneer Press reported that no criminal complaint nor any other further details will be released until after blood tests are returned, and that can take up to a month. 

Broten was a member of the 1980 U.S. Olympic hockey team that finished first in Lake Placid

Broten (No 7) played collegiately for coach Herb Brooks (standing behind Broten) with the Minnesota Golden Gophers, the 1980 U.S. Olympic hockey team, and the 1987 Minnesota North Stars (pictured). Broten was the first American-born player to score 100 points in an NHL season and he played in the league for 17 years, winning the Stanley Cup in New Jersey in 1995

Broten played collegiately for coach Herb Brooks with the Minnesota Golden Gophers. The 5foot9 center would help to win the 1979 NCAA title and later received the inaugural Hobey Baker Award as the country's top college player.

He and Brooks were reunited on team USA in 1980, when the underdog Americans upset the heavily favored Soviet Red Army team in the semifinals en route to a gold medal in Lake Placid, New York. 

Dubbed the 'Miracle on Ice,' the historic upset was later dramatized in the Disney movie, 'Miracle.'

Broten went on to a 17-year NHL career, most of which came with the Minnesota North Stars, including one season under Brooks. 

He was a member of the champion 1995 New Jersey Devils, which makes Broten one of only two players to win an NCAA title, a Stanley cup, and an Olympic gold.

Broten was also the first American-born player to register 100 points (combined goals and assists) in a single season.
