The Top 10 Most Colorful Home Town Kitchens Laurel Mercantile

Publish date: 2024-02-12

Over the last six years, Erin and Ben have transformed 70 homes and buildings on Home Town. We've watched their style and color take on a new form in each episode to match the personalities of those who will live there. It's been a challenge, but we've narrowed down to the top 10 favorite kitchens from the past five seasons. Do you remember any of these kitchens and what house would you add to our list?

The Douglas Home 

Gwen and Mike chose a house where they can entertain friends in a walkable neighborhood. Ben and Erin give the place a complete facelift, add a coastal color scheme, a built-in banquet, and a back patio that’s perfect for entertaining!

The Keith House

Tiffany inherited her mom’s house when she passed away, but at 1500 sq feet, it’s far too small for her growing family. Ben and Erin came up with an ambitious plan for Tiffany and her husband Jonathan to live comfortably with clever color choices and repurposed materials.

The Phillips-Stein House

Rhonda wanted to settle in Mississippi with her husband Jay. While they’ve both taught classes in historic preservation and community planning, they have never owned a historic home together! Erin and Ben transformed their little craftsman into an oasis, full of color and texture.

The Jones House

Richard T. Jones is a Hollywood actor and he and his wife Nancy live in LA for his work, but have fallen in love with Mississippi. They are inspired by Ben and Erin’s work beautifying Laurel and want to make a similar investment in the community to help bring the pride of ownership to areas that haven’t been cared for as much as others. Ben and Erin give the house a colorful makeover adding a classic Craftsman front porch and swing, stunning kitchen and a cozy master bedroom.

The Berry-DeMonte House

Josh & Giorgio wanted to have a vacation home in Mississippi, where Giorgio is originally from. They needed help finding something large enough to entertain, but manageable enough to maintain from their home in Florida. Ben and Erin transformed their new house into the perfect Mississippi vacation getaway and the kitchen into a show stopping centerpiece.

The Turner House

Charlie and Melissa Turner were offered a Manse as part of their compensation with the Ellisville Presbyterian Church. With 5 children and only 3 bedrooms – the place was cramped. With an all in budget of $85,000, Ben and Erin showed them a solid plan to update the Manse and turn it into a living space that would be suitable for their family of 7. Erin and Ben created their dream kitchen and used their go-to blue to make it pop.

The Napier House

Ben's baby brother, Jesse, and his wife Lauren bought their first house, and Ben and Erin are there to guide every step of the way. Drawing their inspiration from Jesse and Lauren's love of eclectic style and bold colors, Ben and Erin chose a dramatic, dark green* for their kitchen. We're still not over it!

The Rasberry House

Danny is one of Laurel's most passionate proponents of restoring old homes. He's also Erin's uncle! Danny likes to go big, and he's not concerned about the budget. Erin and Ben transform his home into a place that reflects his traditional style with modern and textured elements.

THE COCHRAN HOUSE

Lisa and Mike run a non-profit that provides transitional housing for women who are recovering from difficulties. They’re passionate about restoring old houses because they believe that things that are imperfect and broken can be made beautiful again, just like the lives of the women they help. Ben and Erin get scrappy and turn a neglected building into an uplifting oasis of calm and safety where new beginnings can flourish.
