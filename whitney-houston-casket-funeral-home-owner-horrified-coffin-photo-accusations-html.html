Whitney Houston in casket: Funeral home owner 'horrified' over coffin photo accusations

Funeral home owner 'horrified' over accusations she leaked Whitney Houston casket photo

By Ann Pride
Updated: 10:06 EDT, 26 February 2012

17

comments


The owner of the funeral home that handled Whitney Houston's remains is said to be 'devastated' that a photo apparently showing the singer's body in an open casket was leaked to the National Enquirer.

It is believed the picture was taken inside the Whigham Funeral Home in Newark, New Jersey, where Whitney's family attended a private wake last Friday on the eve of her funeral.

But Carolyn Whigham , who owns the business, says neither she nor her staff is responsible.

Gone too soon: Whitney Houston died on 11 February

'We did not take that photo. We did not sell the photo. We would never do something like that,' Whigham told the New York Daily News.

Whigham, who is a second-generation owner of the Whigham Funeral Home said she had a long-standing relationship with Houston.

'Whitney was a friend,' she said. 'I’m the one who flew to Los Angeles and got Whitney from the coroner’s office. I did everything to protect her.'

The image shows the singer's body resting inside her partially open casket.

The National Enquirer has run the snap alongside a bold headline proclaiming: 'Whitney: The last photo!'

When the picture hit newstands, the business owner said she received a number of distressing emails from irate Houston fans and she now fears for her staff’s safety.

'One email said that if the person owned a dog, and the dog died, they would not come to my funeral home,' she said.

Controversial cover: The National Enquirer published a photo that is believed to show Houston's body in a partially open casket

'I’m worried about my employees, worried about me. I’ve been in business since 1943. This is my name, my character. Honestly, this is my life’s work. We would never do something like this.'

Houston's mother Cissy and daughter Bobbi Kristina are said to be outraged about the photo.

'Their heart broken all over again' sources close to the family told TMZ.

Sad goodbye: Houston's family and friends attended her star-studded funeral last Saturday in Newark, New Jersey

One source said 'the family could tell from the photo that it was taken from inside the funeral home, but they have no idea who would have snapped the pic.'

Houston, 48, died in a hotel suite in Beverly Hills on 11 February.

A spokesman for the Los Angeles county coroner said multiple bottles of prescription medication were recovered from her room, but the official cause of her death has not been revealed.

Laid to rest: A hearse carrying the singer's body was escorted to her final resting place last Sunday in Westfield, New Jersey

Paying their respects: Fans gathered at the singer's burial site last weekend

The shocking Enquirer cover photo shows Houston wearing a deep purple dress, a sparkling brooch and what appears to be diamond earrings.

Whigham disputed the supermarket tabloid's claim that Houston was buried with $500,000 worth of precious stones.

'That’s crazy. It’s not true,' she told The Daily News.
