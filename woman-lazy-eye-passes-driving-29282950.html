Woman with lazy eye passes driving test but trolls warn 'stay off the road'

Publish date: 2024-02-11

A woman famed for her lazy eye passed her driving test – but trolls warned her to 'stay off the road'.

Elley Davies, from Pembrokeshire, Wales, was diagnosed with strabismus in her left eye as a child.

After refusing to wear glasses, she is about 80% blind in her left eye and her right side works overtime so she can see.

But that has not stopped the 19-year-old model from achieving her dreams – including learning how to drive.

"I passed my driving test on the 8th of February," Elley gleefully told Daily Star in an exclusive interview.

"Passing felt like a weight lifted off my shoulders, it’s something I’ve dreamed of for so long.

"So the thrill and freedom was unbelievable!

"I had a feeling I was going to pass as my instructor was honestly amazing at calming me down.

"From overthinking situations too much, and believing in myself was my only fault when it came to driving in lessons.

"My instructor knew I would pass, he always told me I was an amazing learner to teach and he knew I wanted this so bad."

Despite Elley passing first time, not everyone is pleased to know she is behind the wheel.

Having battled trolls in their masses on TikTok before, she is no stranger to receiving cruel comments on the videos she shares with her 243,000 followers.

In celebration of passing her test, she shared a clip counting down the hours until she found out the results.

The stunner explained: "When I passed I did a before and after test video, counting down hours till the test and then last clip was me holding certificate.

"The video has +1m views, with people commenting 'stay off the road' or the usual eye joke comments and people asking how I can drive, just stupid questions and comments.

"There’s also [comments] like 'she turns left to go right' and 'bog eye' same old comments.

“I’m okay with hate because there are people who just don’t understand what it’s like to have a visible insecurity, we all have them, mine just happens to be out on show, which makes them think they’ve got the advantage to judge."

Previously, Elley revealed to us that she hoped to become a model before she joined OnlyFans to post saucy snaps for cash.

She explained: “I like the ability to be free with my body on the app.

“It’s not just about selling yourself, it’s about self love and being happy to show the body you love and have.

"Social media is now a big place to build a career for yourself.

"I believe in what I’m doing is setting myself up for a happy life that I choose to live."

Since then Elley has joined a talent agency that looks after influencers – and she's looking forward to what the future has in store for her.
